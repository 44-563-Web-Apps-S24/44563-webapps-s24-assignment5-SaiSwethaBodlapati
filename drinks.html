When reducing the size of the browser window, we can notice the row containing the drink names breaks before the row with the letters.
This occurs because the drink names are longer and require more horizontal space compared to the single-character letters.
As the window width decreases, the available space within each column becomes limited. The longer drink names cannot fully fit within their respective columns and begin to overflow, causing them to wrap onto the next line and breaking the row.
Therefore, the drink names break first due to their length and the limited horizontal space available, while the shorter letters remain intact.
